   430→
   431→**Level 1 — Local Gorakhpur (Start here)**
   432→- Advantage: Zero transport, inspect before buying, no minimum order
   433→- Disadvantage: Price may be 5–12% higher than source state
   434→- Use for: First 3 months until cash flow stabilises
   435→
   436→**Level 2 — Lucknow/Kanpur (Month 3+)**
   437→- Advantage: 8–15% cheaper on spices sourced from Rajasthan/MP
   438→- Transport: ₹2–4/kg on 100kg+ shipments = net saving after transport
   439→- Use for: Orders above ₹15,000 per product
   440→
   441→**Level 3 — Direct from source state (Year 1, Q4+)**
   442→- Nizamabad (Telangana) for turmeric: Direct farmer/processor contact
   443→- Rajasthan for coriander/cumin: Jodhpur/Kota wholesale
   444→- Guntur (Andhra Pradesh) for chilli: Byadagi/Guntur processors
   445→- Only viable when order size exceeds 500kg per commodity
   446→
   447→### Supplier Qualification Checklist
   448→
   449→Before buying from any supplier:
   450→- [ ] Ask for sample (minimum 100g) — test colour, aroma, moisture
   451→- [ ] Check if FSSAI certified
   452→- [ ] Ask for batch date and source origin
   453→- [ ] Negotiate payment: 50% advance, 50% on delivery (or full cash for first deal)
   454→- [ ] Take WhatsApp number — build relationship for repeat orders
   455→- [ ] Compare with at least 2 other suppliers before first large order
